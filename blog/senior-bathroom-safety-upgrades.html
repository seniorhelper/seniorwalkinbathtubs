Bathroom Safety Upgrades for Seniors

Falls Are the #1 Hazard for Seniors in Bathrooms

According to multiple aging-in-place studies, bathrooms are among the highest-risk areas in any home. Slippery floors, high thresholds, and difficult shower access can increase fall risks significantly. Fortunately, there are smart, affordable modifications that can drastically improve safety and independence.

Popular Safety Upgrades

Low Step-In Shower Entry

Replacing traditional tubs with a low threshold or barrier-free shower can dramatically reduce fall risk. Accessible shower entries eliminate the need to step over a tub wall—one of the main causes of slips.

Built-In Grab Bars

Grab bars provide support during transfers and prevent falls. They should be strategically placed next to the toilet, inside a shower, and near any bathing seating area.

Non-Slip Flooring

Textured flooring provides critical traction and prevents slips. Non-slip vinyl or specialized shower bases are commonly used for senior safety upgrades.

Handheld Shower Wand

A handheld shower wand improves convenience by allowing bathing from a seated position—great for limited mobility or wheelchair transfers.

Roll-In Shower Benefits

  • Wheelchair accessibility
  • Lower risk during transfers
  • Improved space inside the shower
  • Safe seated bathing options

Roll-in showers are a top solution for long-term independence and are often ADA compliant in design and safety layout.

Should You Consider a Walk-In Tub?

Walk-in tubs provide seated hydrotherapy benefits and eliminate the need to navigate slippery standard tubs. Many homeowners combine walk-in tubs with accessible showers for complete bathing options.

  • Heated water massage
  • Low step-in entry
  • Built-in grab bars
  • Anti-slip seating

Cost-Effective Install Options

You don’t always need a full bathroom remodel. Barrier-free shower units and many walk-in tubs are designed for retrofit installation, meaning installation may require minimal modification. Costs depend on the model, plumbing location, and installation accessibility.
